Regulation

RBI guidelines on international money transfer.

Every outward remittance from India is governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) and the RBI's Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS). Here's a clean read of what residents can send, for which purposes, and the paperwork that goes with each.

Annual LRS limit
USD 2,50,000 per resident individual, per financial year

Money2Abroad's remittance partners operate as Authorised Dealer Category-II entities, permitted to facilitate outward remittances against the 17 RBI-notified purposes. All transactions are routed through RBI-licensed AD-I scheduled banks; customer money never sits on our balance sheet.

The Liberalised Remittance Scheme was introduced in 2004 and revised periodically. Under the current framework, every resident individual , including minors , may remit up to USD 2,50,000 or equivalent each financial year (April–March) for any permissible current or capital account transaction.

The 17 permitted purposes

An outward remittance under LRS is allowed only against one of these RBI-notified purposes. Each carries its own KYC and document checklist.

What is not permitted

The following transactions are prohibited under LRS, regardless of amount:

  • Remittance for any purpose specifically prohibited under Schedule-I of the FEM (CAT) Rules, 2000.
  • Margin or margin calls to overseas exchanges or counterparties.
  • Purchase of FCCBs issued by Indian companies in the secondary market.
  • Trading in foreign exchange abroad.
  • Remittance to FATF-identified non-cooperative countries and territories.
  • Remittance to entities identified as a significant terrorism risk by the RBI.

Universal documents required

Irrespective of the purpose, every outward remittance under LRS needs the following baseline paperwork:

  • Form A2 , application-cum-declaration under FEMA.
  • LRS declaration confirming cumulative remittances in the financial year are within the USD 2,50,000 limit.
  • PAN card of the remitter (mandatory for all outward remittances).
  • Valid passport of the remitter and, where applicable, the beneficiary.
  • KYC verification certified by the AD branch , typically a recent bank statement, address proof and photograph.
  • Purpose-specific supporting documents as set out below.

How Money2Abroad processes the remittance

  1. Your agency raises the request inside the workspace with the purpose code selected.
  2. Customer KYC, PAN and supporting documents are uploaded; the portal pre-fills Form A2 and the LRS declaration.
  3. The AD-I partner bank verifies eligibility, applies GST and TCS where applicable, and locks the rate.
  4. Customer pays via NEFT / RTGS or approved online flow.
  5. SWIFT remittance is initiated; status updates and SWIFT GPI references are returned to the workspace.
