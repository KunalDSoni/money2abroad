GST calculator for foreign exchange.

India levies GST on the taxable value of forex transactions — currency purchase, forex card load and outward remittance. The tax is computed in slabs, not on the full amount. Use the calculator below to see what your transaction will attract.

Handling and delivery charges are taxed separately at 18% GST.

How the GST is computed.

GST is charged on the "taxable value" of the forex transaction, defined under the CGST Rules. The taxable value rises in tiers based on the rupee value of the deal.

Transaction amount (INR)GST on taxable value
Up to ₹1,00,0000.18% of amount (minimum ₹45)
₹1,00,001 to ₹10,00,000₹180 + 0.09% of amount exceeding ₹1,00,000
Above ₹10,00,000₹990 + 0.018% of amount exceeding ₹10,00,000 (max ₹10,800)

Two common scenarios.

Rupee value of the deal₹4,12,900

Example 2 — Purchase of USD 12,000 @ ₹88.35

Rupee value of the deal₹10,60,200
GST on first ₹1,00,000 (flat)₹180.00
GST on next ₹9,00,000 @ 0.09%₹810.00
GST on excess ₹60,200 @ 0.018%₹10.84
Total GST₹1,000.84
Notes
  • GST is charged on the taxable value, not on the full rupee value of the transaction.
  • For amounts up to ₹25,000, the effective minimum GST is ₹45.
  • Indicative figures only — final GST is calculated by the dealing AD bank at the time of settlement.
  • TCS (Tax Collected at Source) is separate from GST. See our TCS calculator.
